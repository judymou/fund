Carla Gilmore, a retired teacher from California, now helps run computer labs in Mexico and needs help buying three new laptops for her students.

Carla Gilmore was a multimedia teacher for 30 years at Cerritos High School in California. After retiring in Mexico, she started volunteering at two computer labs in Chuburna Puerto, a fishing village in the state of Yucatan.

How are the current computers?

The computer lab at the junior high school has 10 computers that serve 200 kids. The community lab has 10 computers that serve 40 to 50 kids per day. On average, two to three kids share a computer together.

Ms. Gilmore expresses her frustration, “The laptops we’re using are old, between 4 to 12 years. There’s a constant need for replacement parts, but they still randomly freeze. Homework progress are lost because the computer randomly restarts.”

How important are the computer labs to students?

The kids here need these computers. More teachers are giving online assignments. Textbooks are no longer enough. Many good resources are online, but most kids do not have computer and internet access at home. The average daily income here is $20. Overfishing has also eliminated many jobs. Alcoholism can be as high as 70% among the men.

These new laptops will help the labs replace old computers, significantly impact students’ ability to complete their homework, and provide access to the internet for the Chuburna Puerto community.
